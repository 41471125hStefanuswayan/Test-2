By looking at these diagrams, we can conclude that this dataset suggests that preparatory and socioeconomic factors have the strongest correlation with academic achievement. Parental Education: Higher average test scores in all subjects are generally linked to higher parental education levels. Lunch Program: Participants on the free/reduced program performed worse than those who paid for a standard lunch, which may indicate a higher socioeconomic status. Test Preparation: After completing a test preparation course, average scores for all three subjects increased noticeably and significantly. These results suggest that, in addition to innate ability, socioeconomic background, as indicated by parental education and lunch status, and access to preparation materials may have a substantial impact on a participant's performance on these tests.
